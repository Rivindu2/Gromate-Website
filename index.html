Literature Survey

Tomato, a commercially valuable crop, is being grown in various regions of Sri Lanka, including wet, dry, and intermediate zones.

Tomato production growth in Sri Lanka

According to the HORDI Department of Agriculture, tomato is a significant crop in Sri Lanka that is grown in many agroclimatic zones except the up-country wet zones. And is an important cash crop with good potential for export. According to Foastat, tomato production in Sri Lanka peaked in 2018. Tomato production in Sri Lanka faced some challenges like a market glut, low profits pests & disease outbreaks. Apart from the climate, terrain, and animal-related damages. According to the Asian Farmers Association, dodamkumbura farmers sell 3,000 kilograms of tomatoes every day for a price set by traders which is often lower than it is for other vegetables. With how comparatively difficult it is to grow healthy tomatoes than it is for other vegetables sometimes farmers are not bothered to harvest tomatoes and leave them to decay.

Defects in crops can be diagnosed using visual mediums via its roots, leaves, and stems. We can use these visual symptoms along with technology for a technology-assisted diagnosis. A few years ago, a plant disease diagnosis was developed using machine learning and image processing techniques to detect plant defects [2]. Traditionally this kind of diagnosis was done manually by experts who had either studied or had experience in farming or more accurate identification systems for diseases, like PCR-based molecular techniques, which have high popularity because of their high sensitivity and reproducibility [3]. An average planter would not satisfy either of the above. These technologies are useful because it would use data and information that experts know to provide faster diagnosis on a large scale.

Research Gap

Identification and Classification of defect

User inputs will be meticulously analysed and deduced the root cause of the defect through a series of intricate processing stages. These subsequent analytical steps will assist in pinpointing the exact cause of the defect, allowing users to gain a thorough understanding of what went wrong. This in-depth analysis enables precise diagnosis. 

Severity and Spread Calculation

Through the system, users will gain an understanding of the severity of their defect. Users can make informed decisions about the necessary actions and remedies if the severity of the defect is clearly assessed. This knowledge will enable them to effectively prioritise and address the defect, whether minor adjustments or more extensive corrective measures are required. It enables users to take appropriate steps and efficiently allocate resources to resolve the defect in a timely manner.

Solution and information

The system will provide users with a comprehensive set of solutions and countermeasures to address tomato crop issues.Furthermore, the system will be a valuable resource, providing important insights into tomato cultivation. Users can find information on planting, watering, fertilising, and harvesting, as well as general care for their tomato plants. This comprehensive approach will provide users with the knowledge and tools they need to produce thriving, bountiful tomato harvests.

Mobile Integrated

Most implmentations that have integrated similar technologies have targeted large scale farmers and machinery. This solution is implemented to a mobile phone to make it easily accessible to any farmer

Research Questions
  • How to classify nutrient deficiencies of tomato plants?
  • How to diagnose water Imbalances in tomato plants ?
  • How to identify burns of fertilizer over-usage ?
  • How to determine sufficient fertilizer amount?
  • How to determine pesticide burns?
Proposed System

The proposed system answers the above questions. This was created with home backyard gardeners in mind. While there have been several systems that detect and diagnose crop issues using mobile-based platforms, they have primarily served large-scale farmers, with a focus on disease prevention and fertiliser management. However, we identified a market gap in which home gardeners face a unique set of challenges in nurturing their crops. Our proposed system fills that void by providing a comprehensive solution for enthusiasts and hobbyists.

System includes a wide range of functionalities, allowing users to address a variety of issues that can affect tomato plants. When a user notices any abnormalities or irregularities in their crops, they can simply take a picture of the affected area, whether it's the fruit or the leaf with the defect. The system will then analyse the image immediately and provide a comprehensive diagnosis of the cause and severity of the problem. Notably, the system's underlying logic is hosted in a cloud-based infrastructure, and the mobile application is built with the Flutter framework. This technology choice ensures that users, regardless of platform or device, can easily access and use the system, resulting in a consistent and user-friendly experience.

Research Objectives
Classification of Nutrient deficiency

The main objective of this component is the classification of major nutrient deficiencies of the tomato plant. Through the proposed mobile application users can identify Nitrogen (N) and Phosphorus (P) deficiencies and their deficiency levels by capturing or uploading an image of the upper surface of a tomato leaf.

Identify the suitable amount of fertilizer based on the soil conditions.

Based on the output from the deficiency classification user can determine based on location and the results which fertilizer him/her plant requires

Classification of injuries due to misapplication of chemicals or over usage of fertilizers.

The main objective of this component is the identification and classification of tomato leaf burns and its severity level of the tomato plant. Through the proposed mobile application users can identify leaf burns and their severity levels by capturing or uploading an image of a tomato leaf

Identify water imbalances in tomato plants and its severity

The main objective of this component is to identify the contribution of microorganisms in the soil for better crop growth and identify the suitable amount of fertilizer. Must be reached several sub-objectives to achieve the proposed component’s main objective.

Methdology
Figure: Methodology Logic

The Proposed system provides 3 main services and 1 sub service

  1. Nutrient deficiency classification
    1. Identify the suitable amount of fertilizer based on the soil conditions
  2. Water-related defects identification.
  3. Classification of injuries due to misapplication of chemicals or over usage of fertilizers.

The following methodology figure ilustrates how the serverside and model logic is use to provide user with simple understandable results.

For an authenticated user, the process begins with uploading an image of a specific plant part (such as a leaf or fruit) based on the selected service. The uploaded images are transmitted to a Flask server, which employs various models falling into three categories to extract relevant information. The server initially validates the uploaded image. If multiple parts of the plant are detected, an algorithm is applied to determine the most defective plant part and the one most centrally positioned in the image. Once the part is identified, it is segmented using the SAM model provided by Meta. The segmented result is then passed through a trained YOLOv8 model to diagnose the presence and extent of any defects. This process involves determining the affected area of the plant part. Using the calculated areas of the plant part and the affected area, the system calculates the spread of the defect. An image is generated, highlighting the identified defect area. This image is then uploaded to Google Storage and provided to the user as part of a JSON response for their review. These results and images are also stored in Firebase for future reference. For users of the nutrient deficiencies service, based on the results obtained from defect detection and classification, they can further access a sub-service to determine the appropriate fertilizers to address the identified issues.
